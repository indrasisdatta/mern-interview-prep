Regulatory & Compliance Frameworks
    21 CFR Part 11: The FDA's "Electronic Records and Electronic Signatures" rule.

    EU Annex 11: The European equivalent focusing on computerized systems.

    GxP: An umbrella term for "Good Practice" (e.g., GMP for Manufacturing, GCP for Clinical Trials, GLP for Labs).

    ALCOA+: The core principles of Data Integrity (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate).

    HIPAA / GDPR: Regulations for protecting Patient Health Information (PHI) and Personal Identifiable Information (PII).
    
Regulatory Bodies
    FDA (Food and Drug Administration): The US regulator.
    EMA (European Medicines Agency): The EU regulator.
    MHRA: The UK regulator.
    Health Canada: The Canadian regulator.

GxP: Good Practice guidelines and regulations. The main goal is patient safety and data integrity.
    Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) - ensuring drugs are consistently produced and controlled according to quality standards  
    Good Clinical Practice (GCP) - patient portals or data capture tools 
    Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) - sample management software 

ALCOA: To satisfy GxP in UI/UX, we follow ALCOA principles. 
 - Attributable: who did it? Every save and submit to have user id. Apply RBAC 
 - Legible: can it be read? 
    Use standard fonts and ensure data exported or displayed in UI work for all screen sizes.
 - Contemporaneous: record timestamp server timezone 
 - Original: is it the source? (avoid first writing on paper and typing later)
 - Accurate: is it correct? 
   Use form validations, use dropdown & radio buttons wherever possible instead of textbox  

Data Integrity & Safety
These concepts dictate how the UI must behave to ensure the data is "legal."

    ALCOA+: The gold standard for data. Data must be Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, Complete, Consistent, Enduring, and Available.

    Audit Trail: A chronological record of "who did what and when." If a user changes a dosage from 5mg to 10mg, the Audit Trail must capture that change.

    Electronic Signature (eSig): A legally binding signature. It usually requires two components (e.g., Username and Password) and a "Meaning of Signature" (e.g., "I am the author").

    SOP (Standard Operating Procedure): A written set of instructions that users follow. Your UI design should ideally mirror the steps in the SOP.
