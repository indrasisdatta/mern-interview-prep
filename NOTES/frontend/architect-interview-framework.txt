GAPS: Implementation-focused rather than Strategy-focused. Explanations 
  often assume a "happy path" (e.g., assuming backward compatibility). 
  Needs to bridge the gap between "How it works" and "Why it serves the 
  business/team.
  
[S] SCOPE: Define boundaries/ask questions.
    "Before deciding, I need to know: Is this for a 50-person team or 
    a startup? Is SEO or Bundle Size the primary KPI?"

[T] TRADE-OFFS: Present the conflict clearly.
    "We have two paths: Option A gives us speed but hits the bundle size. 
    Option B is cleaner but adds 3 weeks to the timeline."

[A] ANALYSIS: Compare using specific metrics.
    "From a DX (Developer Experience) and LCP (Performance) standpoint, 
    Option B wins because..."

[R] RECOMMENDATION: Make a firm choice with a mitigation plan.
    "Given our 2-week constraint, I recommend Option A today, with a 
    technical debt ticket to migrate in Q3."


ARCHITECT-LEVEL "POWER TIPS"
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
- THE "THIRD WAY": Always look for a middle ground (e.g., using 
  esbuild-loader inside Webpack instead of a full Vite migration).
- DISCOVERY QUESTIONS: Ask "Is our goal saving server costs or 
  maximizing UI responsiveness?" before suggesting a tech stack.
- ACKNOWLEDGE DOWNSIDES: Every choice has a cost. "We lose Redux 
  DevTools but gain 50% development speed with Zustand."
- CONNECT TO BUSINESS: Mention "Independent Deployments" or 
  "Reduced Blast Radius" instead of just "Cleaner Code."
